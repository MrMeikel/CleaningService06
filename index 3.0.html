How to choose the right cleaning company

A clean office impacts productivity, health, and client impressions.

When choosing a cleaning company, look for these key indicators of quality and reliability:

  • Years of experience and family-owned reputation
  • Proper licensing and insurance coverage
  • Trained, vetted, and consistent team members
  • Transparent quotes — no hidden fees
  • References and verifiable reviews

Don't just go with the cheapest option — go with the one you can trust to be in your space, week after week.

Residential

Deep cleaning vs regular cleaning

Wondering which service your home needs? We break it down.

Regular cleaning maintains your home — surfaces, vacuuming, dusting, kitchens, and bathrooms. It's perfect for weekly or bi-weekly upkeep.

Deep cleaning goes further: behind appliances, inside cabinets, baseboards, grout, ceiling fans, light fixtures, and detailed scrubbing. It's recommended:

  • Once or twice a year for maintenance
  • Before moving in or out
  • After renovations or construction
  • When recurring cleaning hasn't happened in a while

Tips

5 cleaning mistakes that damage your carpet

Common cleaning habits actually shorten your carpet's life.

Most carpet damage comes from common mistakes that seem harmless. Avoid these:

  • Scrubbing stains aggressively — damages fibers. Blot instead.
  • Too much water or detergent — causes mold and residue buildup.
  • Skipping regular vacuuming — dirt grinds fibers down faster.
  • DIY rental machines without training — often trap water.
  • Walking on damp carpet — compresses fibers permanently.

A professional deep extraction once a year — combined with regular vacuuming — keeps carpets looking new for much longer.
